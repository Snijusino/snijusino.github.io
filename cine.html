That was my first trauma. It became a root file in my system—a file that can never be deleted. Every time someone bullied me, every time my family or friends turned on me, that file would execute. My mouth would shut. Even when I had the truth on my side, I couldn't speak. When I finally tried to tell my family how I felt, they called it "drama." They called me "weak." The Black Screen and the First Script

I opened a terminal for the first time—a black screen with a blinking cursor. It didn't judge me. It didn't call me weak. I taught myself to code. I wrote a script, shared it with a community, and for the first time in my life, I felt like I was something.

I even built a high-level encryption/destruction script for a group I won't name. I saw the power I had. I realized my brain didn't just learn; it understood the architecture of the digital world. But as I upgraded my skills, the real world was waiting to pull me back in.
